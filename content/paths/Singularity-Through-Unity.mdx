How can collaboration, or isolation, shape the way we write? From personal residences, to boarding houses and hotels, Jose Marti traveled across New York City writing, collaborating, and influencing culture throughout the 18th-century while contributing his work to the Spanish Language Press. This path will follow the hidden thread between his writing in each location; how they compare, contrast, and what that meant for society at the time.

While photos of his exact bedrooms may not exist, we can collect context clues, articles, relevant politics, and location accuracy to explore how Marti was influenced through his most personal possession: where he laid his head to rest. 

As a writer, who you surround yourself with and where you choose to stay directly impacts what biases you bring to your writing, no matter how central you believe yourself to be. While residences may be more personal, invoking themes of personal relation and reflection, collaborative spaces like hotels and boarding houses may bring thoughts of cultural significance, political turmoil, and collaboration to the forefront. Through this path, we will explore how Marti’s living arrangements influenced his contributions to the Spanish Language Press, and how that impacts the way we learn about him today.
