      Christ Church at 33 Ann Street was an important place in the early
      development of Spanish-speaking religious and intellectual life in New
      York City. The church was led by Félix Varela and it became a place of
      worship, education, and building community. Through the community building
      Christ Church, it helped foster the exchange of ideas that would later
      shape early Spanish-language publications. As part of this Path, Christ
      Church represents how community institutions laid the groundwork for the
      emergence of a Spanish-language public sphere.
---

This project, Building a Voice: The Growth of Spanish-Language Journalism in Early New York, explores how early Spanish-language publications in New York City grew out of religious, cultural, and intellectual spaces. Focusing on the influence of Félix Varela, it maps key sites that reveal the growth of early Spanish-language press
