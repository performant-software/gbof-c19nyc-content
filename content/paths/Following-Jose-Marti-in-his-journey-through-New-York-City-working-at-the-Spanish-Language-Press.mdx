      Jose Marti lived at this boarding house in 1881, this became the second
      location he lived at during his time in New York. At this boarding house,
      Marti began to start sending articles to the newspaper “La Opinión
      Nacional of Caracas”, staring his career.


      Historical Context


      In this newspaper, Marti published a piece of writing named Letter from
      New York, from which he left his signature anonymous as “M. de: Z.”, until
      four months later. He did however, leave the words "from our
      correspondent" under the letter he wrote, which indicated that the writer
      resided in New York. There are rumors as to why he did this, some suggest
      Marti was worried about displeasing the Venezuelan president Antonio
      Guzmán Blanco.
