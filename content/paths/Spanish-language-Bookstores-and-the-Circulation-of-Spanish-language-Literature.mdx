      **Description:**


      This location, at 40 and 42 Broadway, specialized in Spanish and Latin
      American books. Additionally, this location housed the printing offices of
      Nestor Ponce de León - where the Cuban author and activist laid the
      groundwork for Spanish Printing Presses in New York


      **Historical Importance, Context:**


      This is the third and final location of Nestor Poncé de León’s bookstore
      and was known to be crucial for the distribution of Spanish-Language
      literature. In a time period with limited Spanish-langauge bookstores and
      publishers, León's establishment(s) allowed for the circulation of many
      genres: fiction, non fiction, political, and more.


      **Significance to Pathway and Works Cited:**


      The final site in this pathway wraps up the steady relocating of Nestor
      Poncé de León’s publishing offices. Understanding the growth and movement
      of publishers and writers is essential when analysing how Spanish-language
      literature expanded across New York City. Having not one, not two, but
      three separate locations shows the dedication and tenacity that describes
      19th century Latin activists and their efforts towards inclusivity and
      solidarity.
