The majority of Hispanic communities were part of the Catholic faith, where religion played an important role in their culture. During this time in New York City, these communities strived to create a sense of belonging and develop spaces for communication. Religious institutions provided a safe environment where people could gather and build community. By highlighting St. Peter’s Church and Spanish-language publications in this tour, it becomes clear how these spaces positively impacted individuals within these groups. Throughout the city, despite their differences, churches and newspapers created an outlet for Spanish-speaking individuals, especially those who deeply valued religion.
