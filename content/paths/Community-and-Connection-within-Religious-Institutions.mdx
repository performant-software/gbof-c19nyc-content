      #### St. Peter’s Church


      Description: 


      St. Peter's Church is a Catholic church located in Lower Manhattan. I
      served as an institution for many members of the Spanish speaking
      community to gather and worship.


      Historical Context:


      During the nineteenth century, many Hispanic immigrants in New York were
      apart of the Catholic religion. St. Petters church was somewhere people
      could meet others and build new relationships that shared the same faith.


      Significance:


      This shows how religion helped create a sense of community within the
      area. it highlights how a shared religion brought people together and
      supported the development of the Hispanic community.

      #### El Mensajero Semanal de Nueva York


      Description:


      El Mensajero Semanal de Nueva York was a Spanish-language newspaper
      published in New York City. This newspaper provided news and information
      to Spanish speaking communities living in New York.



      Historical Context:


      During this time newspapers were a major way of people stayed informed and
      connected with the things going on in the world. Especially for Spanish
      speaking communities this Spanish language newspaper helped them immensely
      in keeping up with the political, cultural, and social issues.



      Significance:


      This represents how print media connected people throughout the city. It
      shows how this newspapers helped build communities and strengthened the
      communication within the Spanish speaking communities across the city.
