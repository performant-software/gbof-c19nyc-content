This path dives in to the emergence of Spanish-language publishing in New York City showing the people, places, and networks that brought it to life. It begins at St. Peter’s Church (22 Barclay), where Félix Varela used the church as a hub for worship, teaching, and sharing ideas with the Spanish-speaking community. From there, it moves to Christ Church (33 Ann), where the community came together to support their institutions and strengthen their connections. Next, we visit Gray and Bunce Printers (347 Pearl), where newspapers like El Habanero were brought off the press and into the hands of readers. Finally, the path ends with La Verdad, a newspaper that gave Spanish-speaking residents a voice and helped shape early journalism in the city. Together, these stops tell a story of community, communication, and the foundations of Spanish-language media in New York.
