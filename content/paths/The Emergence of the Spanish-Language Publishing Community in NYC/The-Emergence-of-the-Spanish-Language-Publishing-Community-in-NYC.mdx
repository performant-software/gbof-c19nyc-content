    blurb: >
      St. Peter’s Church was where Félix Varela worked when he arrived in New
      York in the 1820s. It became a central hub for both religious and
      intellectual life within the Spanish-speaking community. Even though he
      was a priest, Varela used the church as more than just a place for
      worship, it was where he wrote, taught others, and thought through big
      social and political ideas. It was during his time here that he began
      contributing to early Spanish-language publications, using print as a way
      to communicate ideas about reform, education, and freedom. This site shows
      how closely connected the church was to the development of
      Spanish-language media, serving as a starting point for the growth of
      publishing in New York City
