In the late nineteenth century, Ann Street was lined with print shops, many of which served the neighborhood's Spanish-language press, among other communities of print.

This virtual walk explores several sites connected to the production of Spanish-language newspapers. Together these locations show that the nineteenth-century Spanish-language press in New York depended not only on writers and editors but also on printers, publishers, and booksellers who helped produce and circulate these publications. By examining the geography of these sites, we can begin to see how writers and editors including José Martí and Rafael Serra formed their intellectual community around the practical work of printing newspapers.
