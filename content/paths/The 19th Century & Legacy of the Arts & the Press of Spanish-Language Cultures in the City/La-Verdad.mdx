      **El Americano**


      **Description**


      Despite this publication having no address, El Americano was a platform
      for the people of the Junta Central Republicana including Cuba and Puerto
      Rico. Through these extensive reports, communities were able to support
      one another by connecting political views and cultural beliefs.


      **Historical Context**


      Organizations such as the Junta Central Republicana, advocated for several
      independence movements to unify people against political inequalities
      taking place in Cuba and Puerto Rico.


      **Significance**


      El Americano played a pivotal role in the early development of the Spanish
      press; the lack of information retaining this publication is limited with
      there being no traces of the address nor any images being available on the
      internet. This reflects how many of the Spanish language newspapers at the
      time were short lived and lacked proper platform to vocalize their
      political outlooks.

      La Verdad


      Description


      La Verdad is one of the many publications that could be found at 89 Nassau
      St. This Spanish language newspaper focused primarily on political and
      societal injustices that occured in both the United States and Cuba. As a
      result, Spanish speaking communities were kept updated and engaged while
      adjusting to this foreign world around them.


      Historical Content


      At the time many Latin Americans lived in exile and fear, these newspapers
      allowed an outlet for these quiet voices to be heard to a more broader
      audience. These newspapers played a role to provide as much information as
      possible, especially topics regarding inequalities.


      Significance


      This publication holds significance since it exposes political dilemmas
      while allowing diverse communities to connect ideas and their voice to be
      heard.

Early 19th Century Printing Press![](https://core-data-tina-cms.s3.us-east-1.amazonaws.com/gbof-c19nyc/Screenshot_20260318_114057_Chrome.jpg)This path shows how the evolution of Spanish language publications in the late 1800's assisted in connecting many Latin communities by giving them a proper platform to share their political and cultural beliefs.

This walk highlights publications such as La Verdad, La Revolucion, El Americano and El Mundo Nuevo who distributed valuable information retaining real world events taking place at the time while giving a voice to communities who were often silenced by higher authorities. These newspapers unified people from different countries to spread awareness of these injustices. This resulted these people to foster a strong desire for justice in a time where inequalities were overbearing for the average citizen.
